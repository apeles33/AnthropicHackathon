# Trading Analytics - Key Papers & References

Curated collection of seminal academic papers and industry research for each project type. These papers provide theoretical foundations and practical insights for implementation.

## 1. Momentum Trading

### Seminal Papers

**Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers** (1993)
- Authors: Narasimhan Jegadeesh, Sheridan Titman
- Journal: Journal of Finance
- Key Finding: 3-12 month momentum strategies earn ~1% per month
- Link: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1993.tb04702.x

**On Persistence in Mutual Fund Performance** (1997)
- Author: Mark M. Carhart
- Journal: Journal of Finance
- Contribution: Four-factor model including momentum factor
- Link: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1997.tb03808.x

**Momentum** (2011)
- Authors: Tobias J. Moskowitz, Mark Grinblatt
- Journal: Review of Financial Studies
- Coverage: Industry momentum across 8 countries
- Link: https://doi.org/10.1093/rfs/14.4.1093

## Citation Guidelines

When implementing strategies based on academic papers:

1. **Always cite the original paper** in your HTML output
2. **Link to publicly available versions** (e.g., SSRN, arXiv)
3. **Note key assumptions** that may differ from your implementation
4. **Acknowledge simplifications** made for educational purposes
5. **Reference follow-up work** that improves or critiques the original

Example citation format:
```
This project implements the momentum strategy from Jegadeesh and Titman (1993),
"Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency."
The implementation uses a simplified 6-month lookback period rather than the
cross-sectional portfolio construction in the original paper.
```
